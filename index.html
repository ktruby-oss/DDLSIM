Platform Overview

DDLSim-Lab is an open-source research platform designed to simulate, analyze, and optimize distributed deep learning systems using AI-driven control mechanisms.

The platform enables experimentation with large-scale, heterogeneous, and failure-prone environments, including edge-cloud hybrid infrastructures. It provides researchers with a reproducible, cost-free environment to test novel algorithms, scheduling strategies, and fault-tolerance mechanisms without requiring access to expensive physical testbeds.

Why Bare-Metal is Required

To achieve scientifically valid results, DDLSim-Lab must run on bare-metal infrastructure. Virtualization layers introduce non-deterministic noise. Bare-metal deployment ensures:

  • Elimination of virtualization overhead – Essential for accurate scaling studies.
  • Realistic network behavior – Precise emulation of latency, jitter, packet loss.
  • High-fidelity distributed experiments – Support for SR-IOV, RDMA, and advanced interconnects.
